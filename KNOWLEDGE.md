# Knowledge Index

## Spotify Wrapped Design Principles

This section documents the design patterns, UX principles, and visual characteristics that make Spotify Wrapped successful, to be applied to the Tesco Wrapped dashboard.

### Core Design Philosophy

**Theme: Reinvention & Evolution**
- Celebrates the collision of genres, influences, and unexpected cultural moments
- Reflects how pop culture thrives in a beautiful cycle of constant evolution
- Makes users feel central to cultural momentum
- Personal connection: "when listeners engage, culture shifts"

### Visual Design Characteristics

**Typography as Hero Element**
- Spotify Mix typeface used as the main graphic element (2024 innovation)
- Typography loops, transforms, and celebrates energy
- "Type as Pattern" approach - artful use of number forms
- Bold "hyperloops" of numbers (e.g., 2 and 4) propel forward in vivid technicolor
- Delivers "a bolder, dynamic and more unique typographic presence"

**Color & Visual Energy**
- Dynamic blend of vibrant gradients and solid colors
- Playful layouts with spirited animations
- High-energy color palette that celebrates momentum
- Layers that blend and shift
- Vibrant technicolor combinations

**Animation & Motion**
- Spirited animations breathe life into design
- Typography that loops and transforms
- Variety in pacing: animated panels introduce themes, followed by simple text presentations
- Unexpected jumps between condensed and expanded font iterations

### UX Patterns & Psychology

**Story-Based Navigation**
- Combines familiar patterns from Instagram stories
- Scrollable, swipeable card-based interface
- Sequential revelation of insights

**Shareability by Design**
- Derivative layouts specifically created for social media sharing
- Design with social platforms in mind
- Make features easy and attractive to share at the most shareable moment
- Visual content optimized for screenshots and sharing

**Psychological Engagement**
- **Curiosity Gap**: Build anticipation (notifications sent a week before release)
- **Personalization**: Custom variations that capture personal moments
- **Emotional Connection**: Emotion-led UX that makes users feel special
- **Surprise & Delight**: Unexpected insights and fun facts

**Design Simplicity (2024 Evolution)**
- Fewer design tactics in combination for fresh experience
- Avoids maximalist excess of previous years
- Simple but fresh approach

### Key Takeaways for Implementation

1. **Make It Personal**: Every metric should feel unique to the user
2. **Build Anticipation**: Create moments of surprise and revelation
3. **Design for Sharing**: Every screen should be screenshot-worthy
4. **Use Bold Typography**: Let numbers and text be the visual heroes
5. **Vibrant Colors**: Use gradients and solid colors that pop
6. **Smooth Animations**: Add life through motion and transitions
7. **Tell a Story**: Structure as a narrative journey through the year
8. **Keep It Simple**: Focus on a few strong design elements rather than overwhelming complexity

### Data Structure for Tesco Wrapped

The transaction data includes:
- **Customer Profile**: Name, contact info, preferences
- **Purchase History**: Individual transactions with timestamps
- **Transaction Details**: Store location, basket value, savings, payment method
- **Product Data**: Item names, quantities, prices, weights/volumes
- **Store Information**: Store names, formats (Extra, Express, etc.), addresses

### Potential Metrics to Display

- Total spending across the year
- Most purchased items/categories
- Favorite store locations
- Busiest shopping day/time
- Total savings from deals
- Shopping streaks and patterns
- Fun facts (e.g., "enough milk to fill a bathtub")
- Seasonal shopping trends
- Payment method preferences
- Weekday vs. weekend shopping habits
